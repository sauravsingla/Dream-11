# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful and harassment-free environment for everyone who participates in this project, regardless of experience level, background or identity.

## Expected behaviour

Participants should:

- communicate respectfully and constructively;
- focus criticism on ideas, code, experiments and evidence rather than individuals;
- acknowledge limitations, uncertainty and conflicting results honestly;
- respect data licences, privacy, attribution and research integrity;
- help make discussions accessible to contributors with different levels of expertise.

## Unacceptable behaviour

Harassment, discrimination, personal attacks, intimidation, deliberate disruption, plagiarism, misrepresentation of results, or publication of private information without permission are not acceptable.

## Research integrity

Contributors must clearly distinguish original, reconstructed and synthetic data; document data provenance; disclose material solver or experimental limitations; and avoid overstating reproducibility or performance claims.

## Enforcement

Potential violations may be reported privately using the process described in `SECURITY.md` or through an appropriate private contact channel associated with the repository owner. Reports will be reviewed in good faith and handled as confidentially as reasonably possible.

Project maintainers may edit or remove contributions, comments, issues or other participation that does not align with this code of conduct. Repeated or serious violations may result in temporary or permanent exclusion from project spaces.

## Scope

This code applies in repository discussions, issues, pull requests, reviews and other project-related communication.
